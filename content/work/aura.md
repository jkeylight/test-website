## The Brief

Aura came to us with a product they were proud of and a brand they weren't. Their wellness app had 40,000 active users, genuine clinical evidence behind it, and a visual identity that looked like every other health startup: sterile white, clinical blue, stock photography of people doing yoga in fields.

The brief was simple but difficult: make Aura feel premium without feeling cold. Make it feel scientific without feeling clinical. Make it feel calm without feeling boring.

The benchmark they gave us was a list of luxury lifestyle brands — not other health apps. They wanted to be compared to Aesop, not to Calm. That single instruction told us everything we needed to know.

## Strategy

Before we opened Figma, we spent two weeks in discovery. The insight that changed everything: Aura's users didn't describe the product in clinical terms. They described how it made them feel. "Grounded." "Held." "Like someone is looking after me."

That language became the brief. The brand needed to feel like being held. Warm but not saccharine. Dark enough to feel luxurious but never cold. The visual language we landed on drew from natural materials: aged brass, dark stone, warm candlelight.

## Identity Design

The wordmark uses a lightly customised geometric serif — clean enough to signal modernity, warm enough to signal care. The letter-spacing is generous: breathing room, not clinical distance.

The color palette anchors on a deep charcoal base with a single warm accent — a muted gold that reads as honest and earned, not flashy.

## Technology

- **Framework:** Next.js 14 App Router
- **Animation:** GSAP + ScrollTrigger
- **Scroll:** Lenis
- **Hero:** Canvas 2D animated orb
- **CMS:** Sanity
- **Hosting:** Vercel
- **Performance:** 98 Lighthouse score

## Results

The site went live on a Tuesday morning. By Thursday it was on Awwwards. By the following Monday Aura had hit a record week for new signups.
