A Skeptic Who Found Wisdom

Paul Brunton was born in London in 1898 and began his spiritual journey as a journalist — skeptical, questioning, and determined to separate genuine wisdom from superstition. His travels to India in the 1930s changed everything.

His encounter with Ramana Maharshi at the foot of Arunachala — described in his landmark book 'A Search in Secret India' — is one of the most celebrated meetings of East and West in modern spiritual history.

The Philosophy of the Overself

PB developed a comprehensive system of philosophy he called the 'Philosophy of the Overself' — a synthesis of Western reason and Eastern mysticism.

Unlike many spiritual teachers, PB wrote for the educated, skeptical modern mind. He rejected dogma, demanded rational examination, and insisted that genuine spiritual experience was available to any sincere seeker.

His India Connection

India was not merely a destination for PB — it was the crucible of his transformation. He visited multiple times, studied classical Sanskrit texts under V. Subramanya Iyer in Mysore.
